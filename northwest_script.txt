🎬 Northwest - Pilot Episode: "The Acceptance"
INT. LAWRENCE'S BEDROOM - NIGHT
Lawrence WALKER (18, Black, trim, charismatic but currently radiating anxiety), sits hunched over his sleek, slightly dusty MacBook Pro. His room is impeccably organized—a tribute to his upper-middle-class upbringing—but still has that teenage-boy feel: a faded poster of Kobe Bryant, an expensive gaming console, a stack of college brochures on his nightstand.

It's late. The only light comes from the screen, casting a blue glow on his face. He's on the Georgetown University application portal.

LAWRENCE’s hands hover over the 'View Decision' button.

His phone vibrates on the desk. He glances at it—a text from his best friend, TERRY (18, Black, Lawrence’s height, dressed in a hoodie and joggers, exudes relaxed confidence).

T: Did you hit it yet??

Lawrence sighs, a deep, weary sound, and types back.

L: Nah. I’m waiting for the right vibe. Like, maybe a full moon? Or when I’m less likely to projectile vomit.

T: Bruh. You applied to ten schools. You’re already in at Maryland. Just click the button. I’m on my way over. We can celebrate or mourn.

A beat. Lawrence closes his eyes, takes a deep breath, and finally clicks. The screen refreshes.

MONTAGE: QUICK CUTS

Lawrence’s wide eyes.

A graphic of the Georgetown Hoya mascot.

The word...

INT. LAWRENCE'S BEDROOM - MOMENTS LATER
Lawrence stares at the screen. He lets out a single, sharp exhale of relief, which quickly morphs into a wide, slightly goofy smile.

The door bursts open. TERRY strides in, holding a six-pack of craft soda (since they're still 18).

TERRY
> What’s the word, my dude? Is it a W or an L?

Lawrence slowly turns his laptop to face Terry.

LAWRENCE
> I got in, man.

Terry’s face breaks into a genuine, explosive grin. He drops the soda and rushes to slap hands with Lawrence, then pulls him into a quick, solid embrace.

TERRY
> Northwest, baby! Hoya Saxa! Georgetown! We doing this!

LAWRENCE
> We? You’re going to Howard.

TERRY
> I’m going to the Mecca. You are going to the gentrified Mecca. It's five minutes away. We are basically roommates. The only thing separating us is the color of the campus brochure.

Terry picks up the soda, popping one open.

LAWRENCE
> Hey, look. I know Howard is the move for everyone, but—

TERRY
> (Interrupting, hands up)
> Chill, chill. No shade. But listen, man. You going to Georgetown—a PWI. You know what that means, right?

LAWRENCE
> Yeah. Predominantly White Institution. I took notes.

TERRY
> It means they need you there. They got the stats to keep up. It also means you’re gonna be in classrooms where your opinion is the Black opinion—full stop. You’re gonna be their diversity checkbox.

Lawrence takes a slow sip of his soda, his smile fading slightly as the reality sets in.

LAWRENCE
> I get that. But the Business School is top ten. I want the exposure, the network... I've got to play the game, right? It's the path to that corner office. My dad says—

TERRY
> Your dad’s a great man, and he’s giving you the playbook he used. But Lawrence, that corner office is a myth for us. It’s always going to be an uphill sprint, no matter the pedigree.

Terry sits on the edge of the bed.

TERRY (CONT'D)
> My biggest fear is graduating and having to choose between a job that values my skill and a job that values my skin. You’re just accelerating that decision.

LAWRENCE
> Okay, damn. Way to kill the mood, Terry. I just got in. Can we have five minutes of "I'm a genius" before we pivot to "the struggle"?

TERRY
> My bad. My bad. You a genius! Congratulations, man. Seriously. This is huge.

He raises his soda can.

TERRY (CONT'D)
> To our next chapter. You at Northwest, me at the Hilltop.

LAWRENCE
> (Raising his can)
> To surviving it.

They clink cans. Lawrence looks back at the acceptance screen, a mixture of pride, excitement, and a new, unsettling nervousness washing over him.

FADE OUT.

INT. LAWRENCE'S KITCHEN - MORNING
The kitchen is spacious and modern, fitting Lawrence's upper-middle-class home. His mom, DR. ANNA WALKER, is expertly making breakfast.

CHAD KERR (18, Black, sharp dresser, confident to the point of being cocky) and DEREK DUBOIS (18, Black, quiet, analytical, carries himself with an air of practiced professionalism) are seated at the granite island. Terry is also there, pouring orange juice.

Chad is passionately arguing a point about the NBA playoffs.

CHAD
> Listen, the Lakers dynasty is built on two things: ego and nostalgia. We are in the era of efficiency. The Celtics are the better team, period.

DEREK
> (Without looking up from his phone)
> Efficiency is irrelevant when you don't have a clutch gene. You can run all the algorithms you want, but sometimes you just need a dude who can step up and hit the shot. That's a trait, not a metric.

Lawrence enters, dressed sharply for the Georgetown information session, but looking slightly overwhelmed.

LAWRENCE
> Morning. We all ready for this campus experience?

TERRY
> Ready to watch Lawrence ascend to the land of $6 coffee and white anxieties.

CHAD
> Nah, Georgetown is a power move. The networking alone is worth it. Derek and I were just talking about how you can leverage that for future business ventures.

Chad points a finger at Lawrence.

CHAD (CONT'D)
> But you need to start dressing the part now, Lawrence. Those chinos are too loose. You need to radiate wealth.

DEREK
> (Calmly correcting Chad)
> You need to radiate preparedness. Wealth comes later. You’re going into the business school, Lawrence. You need to look like you’re already an alum coming back for a high-level briefing.

LAWRENCE
> Right. So, prepared wealth. Got it.

DR. WALKER (O.S.)
> All that advice is free, boys. But remember, the first day, the most important thing you project is confidence. You belong there, Lawrence.

Dr. Walker places a plate of eggs and bacon in front of him.

TERRY
> See, that's what I like about Dr. Walker. Straight facts.

DR. WALKER
> And you boys remember what I told you: You represent the best of us. Don't let anyone make you feel small. Now, eat.

EXT. GEORGETOWN UNIVERSITY CAMPUS - DAY
Sunlight dapples through the trees onto the immaculate, old-money campus. The architecture is grand and imposing. Lawrence, Chad, Terry, and Derek look slightly out of place amidst the throngs of well-dressed, predominantly white students and their parents.

They’re waiting outside the McDonough School of Business for a tour. Chad is fiddling with his phone. Derek is adjusting the collar of his shirt.

CHAD
> Man, this place smells like old money and good credit. I respect it.

TERRY
> I respect the architecture. But it feels like a museum where they occasionally let you take a class.

LAWRENCE
> (Looking around, trying to project "preparedness")
> It’s a different vibe, sure. But opportunity looks like this.

A small group of prospective students approaches. Leading the group is CHLOE (18, White, extremely earnest, wearing a large cardigan). She looks nervous and excited.

CHLOE
> Hi! I'm Chloe! I just wanted to say congratulations on getting in, guys. We should all connect!

CHAD
> (Flashing a bright, practiced smile)
> Chad. Good to meet you.

Chloe’s eyes drift immediately to Lawrence, who is wearing his hair in neat, thick coils. She leans in slightly.

CHLOE
> Oh, wow. Your hair is amazing.

LAWRENCE
> Thanks.

CHLOE
> I... I know this is probably a weird question, but... Can I touch it? It just looks so—

Lawrence freezes. A beat of silent, immediate discomfort hits the group. Chad and Derek exchange a knowing, weary look. Terry clenches his jaw.

LAWRENCE
> (His smile stiffens, he tries to laugh it off)
> Oh. Uh... Nah. I’m good. It’s... really fragile. Lot of product in there. You don’t want to mess with the structure.

CHLOE
> (Confused, missing the social cue entirely)
> Oh. Got it. Right. I just have never seen hair like that up close. It’s just so…

Before she can finish, she impulsively reaches out and gives a quick, intrusive SQUEEZE to a patch of hair near Lawrence’s temple before pulling her hand back.

Lawrence’s eyes widen in disbelief. It's the physical embodiment of the boundary being crossed.

CHAD
> (His voice dropping, firm)
> You just touched my man’s hair.

CHLOE
> (She gasps, realizing her transgression)
> Oh my god! I am so sorry! I didn’t—I don’t know why I did that! I was just admiring it!

DEREK
> (Polite but scathing)
> Admiring someone and touching them are two very different concepts, Chloe. Next time, maybe try a compliment that doesn't involve physical contact.

Chloe is mortified. She backs away quickly, stammering apologies.

CHLOE
> I gotta go find my mom. I’m so sorry.

She hurries away. The four friends are left in a loaded silence.

TERRY
> See? Five minutes on campus, and the microaggression counter is already at 'Impulsive Contact.'

CHAD
> (To Lawrence)
> You handled that well. A little too nice, but well. I would've told her to sanitize her hands first.

LAWRENCE
> (He rubs the spot where she touched him, feeling exposed)
> I didn't even know what to say. It felt like I was some kind of zoo exhibit.

DEREK
> Welcome to the PWI experience, Lawrence. You’re exotic until you’re threatening. You need to find your footing.

Introducing the Love Interest: AYANA
As the friends continue walking, Lawrence bumps into someone coming around a corner.

AYANA (18, Black, radiant and effortlessly stylish. She has an athletic build, large glasses, and natural hair pulled into a sleek ponytail. Her energy is warm but assertive—a sharp contrast to Issa Dee’s early-series diffidence.)

AYANA
> Oh, my bad! Heads up.

LAWRENCE
> No, my fault. I was distracted.

Ayana glances at his group, then fixes her gaze on Lawrence.

AYANA
> You must be freshmen tour group. You look a little shell-shocked. Did you just meet the one girl who asks if she can touch your curls?

Lawrence's jaw drops slightly. The relief of being understood is palpable.

LAWRENCE
> You've met her?

AYANA
> They’re everywhere here. My name is Ayana. I’m a sophomore, in the Linguistics program.

LAWRENCE
> Lawrence. I’m starting in the Business School.

AYANA
> McDonough. Good choice. But a word of advice: if anyone asks to touch your hair, tell them you just put deep conditioning treatment in it and it’s a biohazard.

She winks. Ayana’s confidence and sharp wit immediately captivate Lawrence, offering him a brief, comfortable refuge from the awkwardness.

AYANA (CONT'D)
> Anyway, I gotta run. I’m late for a T-shirt design meeting for the Black Student Alliance. Good luck with the tour. Try not to let them touch anything else.

She smiles and walks away with a confident, purposeful stride.

TERRY
> Okay. She is fire. And she’s got a solution for the hair thing. That’s a keeper.

CHAD
> (Nodding approvingly)
> Black Student Alliance? She’s connected. Good look, Lawrence.

DEREK
> (Slightly analytical)
> Assertive and politically engaged. A good foil for your reserved nature. Go get her number, Lawrence.

Lawrence stares after Ayana, completely forgetting the hair incident. His mission has shifted.

LAWRENCE
> I didn't even think to ask... I gotta find her.

FADE OUT.

INT. LEAVEY CENTER - STUDENT UNION - LATER THAT DAY
The student union is a hive of activity. It’s a mix of students in Georgetown hoodies, athletes in tech-fleece, and a few students who look like they’re headed to a Senate hearing.

Lawrence, Chad, Derek, and Terry walk through the lobby. Lawrence is checking his phone, looking at a digital campus map.

CHAD
> I’m telling you, the "Black Student Alliance" is going to be in the basement. It’s always the basement. They give us the space with the most pipes and the least sunlight.

DEREK
> (Checking a directory)
> Actually, it’s Room 402. Top floor. Better views, higher stakes.

TERRY
> See? Progress. Even if it’s just vertical.

Lawrence stops at a large glass trophy case, adjusting his shirt in the reflection. He looks nervous.

LAWRENCE
> Alright, look. If she’s in a meeting, I don’t want to be that guy. The "thirsty freshman" guy.

CHAD
> Lawrence, look at me. You are a Georgetown Man now. Or, like, a Georgetown Man-in-waiting. You aren't "thirsty," you’re "showing initiative." In the business world, that’s called a follow-up.

DEREK
> Just don’t over-calculate. You’re looking for a reason to be there.

LAWRENCE
> (Nodding)
> Right. A reason. Like... I’m interested in the T-shirt design?

TERRY
> (Laughing)
> You? You wore a shirt with a mustard stain to prom, Lawrence. Don’t lie to that girl. Just tell her you wanted to say thanks for the "hair warning." It’s honest.

INT. FOURTH FLOOR - OUTSIDE ROOM 402 - CONTINUOUS
The vibe up here is different. Through the glass doors of the meeting room, they see about fifteen Black students. The energy is high—music is playing low (something soulful but upbeat), and there are sketches of T-shirt designs pinned to a corkboard.

Lawrence spots AYANA. She’s wearing her glasses now—thick, stylish frames—and has an iPad in one hand, gesturing toward a design on the board. She looks commanding and brilliant.

LAWRENCE
> (Whispering)
> There she is.

CHAD
> (Peering through the glass)
> Oh, she’s the one running the show? Okay, Lawrence. You’re playing on "Hard Mode." I like it.

DEREK
> (Noticing the room)
> Look at the room, though. It’s organized. They have an agenda. This isn’t just a hangout; it's a brand.

Suddenly, the door swings open. A tall, athletic guy with a sharp fade walks out, nearly bumping into the four of them. This is MARCUS (20, Black, confident, wears a varsity jacket). He looks at the group of freshmen with a mixture of amusement and "who are you?"

MARCUS
> Can I help you guys? You looking for the gym? Or are you lost on your way to orientation?

CHAD
> (Quickly)
> We’re looking for the BSA meeting. My friend here—

Chad claps a hand on Lawrence’s shoulder.

CHAD (CONT'D)
> —is a graphic design prodigy. He heard there was a T-shirt situation?

Lawrence glares at Chad. Ayana looks up from the board and notices them. She recognizes Lawrence and walks over, a small, knowing smirk on her face.

AYANA
> (To Marcus)
> It’s okay, Marcus. I know him. He’s the one the "Hair-Touchers" tried to claim earlier.

The room lets out a collective "Oooooh" and a few laughs. Marcus relaxes, nodding at Lawrence.

MARCUS
> Tough break, man. Welcome to the Hilltop.

Ayana steps out into the hallway, leaning against the doorframe. She looks Lawrence up and down, her gaze lingering on his face. She’s not intimidated; if anything, she’s evaluating him.

AYANA
> So, Lawrence the Business Major. Are you really a "graphic design prodigy," or did your friend just panic?

LAWRENCE
> (Rubbing the back of his neck, leaning into his "unsure but confident" charm)
> Definitely the panic. I’m actually terrible at design. I just... I realized I didn't get a chance to say thank you. For the advice. It actually came in handy two minutes after you left.

AYANA
> (Raising an eyebrow)
> Another one?

LAWRENCE
> Not a touch, but a "Your culture is so vibrant."

Ayana laughs—a genuine, warm sound.

AYANA
> See? It’s a minefield out here. You gotta have your guard up.

TERRY
> (Chiming in)
> That’s what I’ve been telling him! I’m Terry. I’m the "voice of reason" who’s going to Howard so I don’t have to deal with this.

AYANA
> (To Terry)
> Smart move. (Back to Lawrence) Well, since you’re here, and clearly "un-talented" in design, you want to see what we’re actually working on? We could use a "freshman perspective" on the homecoming merch.

LAWRENCE
> (Smiling)
> I’d love to.

CHAD
> (To the group)
> We’ll just... wait out here. Or go find the vending machines.

DEREK
> (To Lawrence)
> Take notes, Lawrence.

Lawrence follows Ayana into the room. As he walks past Marcus, Marcus gives him a "good luck" pat on the back.

INT. MEETING ROOM - CONTINUOUS
Lawrence stands by Ayana as she points to a design of a fist made out of Hoya-blue ivy.

AYANA
> We’re trying to balance the "Georgetown Elite" vibe with some actual Black consciousness. It’s a tightrope walk. You ever feel like that, Lawrence? Like you’re playing two characters at once?

Lawrence looks at her, struck by how quickly she got to the core of his current anxiety.

LAWRENCE
> Every time I put on these chinos.

She grins.

AYANA
> Good. At least you’re self-aware. That’s rare for a freshman. Give me your phone.

Lawrence hands it over, puzzled. She types something in and hands it back.

AYANA (CONT'D)
> That’s my number. Text me when you survive your first week. If you’re still "unsure," I’ll buy you a coffee and tell you which professors to avoid.

She turns back to the board, leaving Lawrence standing there, phone in hand, feeling like he just won a game he didn't even know he was playing.

FADE OUT.

INT. HARBIN HALL - DORM ROOM - DAY
The room is small. Smaller than Lawrence’s walk-in closet at home. Two twin-XL beds, two desks, and a view of a brick wall.

CHAD is struggling with a heavy box of sneakers. DEREK is precisely measuring the distance between the bed and the desk with a tape measure. TERRY is sitting on the bare mattress, judging the "vibe."

CHAD
> (Wheezing)
> Lawrence, why do you have so many hardback books? This is the digital age, bruh. My spine is screaming "lawsuit."

LAWRENCE
> My dad said a physical library shows "intellectual permanence."

TERRY
> Your dad said that because he didn't have to carry them up three flights of stairs. Also, this room is... cozy. And by cozy, I mean I’ve seen prison cells on The Wire with more personality.

DEREK
> It’s about the zip code, not the square footage. You’re three minutes from the waterfront.

The door swings open. In walks LOGAN (18, White, wearing a Vineyard Vines vest and boat shoes, looking like he just stepped off a yacht). He’s followed by a professional moving crew carrying high-end monitors and a mini-fridge.

LOGAN
> Oh, hey! You must be Lawrence! I’m Logan. My dad said the roommate selection was "randomized," but I think he might’ve made a call.

Logan looks at the trio of Black men in the room. His smile falters for a micro-second—not out of malice, but pure, unadulterated "suburban surprise"—before bouncing back.

LOGAN (CONT'D)
> Whoa, you brought the whole squad! That’s awesome. Very... urban of you.

The room goes dead silent. Chad drops a box. Terry looks like he’s about to start a podcast episode right there.

LAWRENCE
> (Tight smile)
> These are my friends. Terry, Chad, and Derek.

LOGAN
> (Vigorously shaking hands)
> High-fives all around! So, Lawrence, I brought a Nespresso machine and a Peloton. Hope you don't mind the "whirring" at 5:00 AM. I’m training for a triathlon.

CHAD
> (To Lawrence, sotto voce)
> If he mentions a "Gap Year in Bali," we’re leaving.

INT. MCDONOUGH SCHOOL OF BUSINESS - LECTURE HALL - TWO DAYS LATER
The hall is a sea of neutral colors—creams, navys, and greys. Lawrence sits in the middle row, his notebook open, pen poised. He’s trying to look like the "prepared wealth" Derek talked about.

PROFESSOR WHITTAKER (50s, White, sharply dressed) stands at the front, clicking through a PowerPoint on "Market Disruption."

PROFESSOR WHITTAKER
> Now, when we look at the revitalization of urban centers—what the media calls "gentrification"—we see a unique intersection of capital and community.

Whittaker scans the room. His eyes lock onto Lawrence.

PROFESSOR WHITTAKER (CONT'D)
> Lawrence, isn't it? You’re from the area. From a... diverse neighborhood?

Lawrence feels the collective "turn" of sixty pairs of eyes. He wasn't even raising his hand.

LAWRENCE
> Uh, yeah. Silver Spring.

PROFESSOR WHITAKER
> Excellent. From your perspective, does the influx of high-end retail represent a loss of "soul," or is it simply the natural evolution of an under-leveraged market? Give us the "ground-level" view.

Lawrence shifts in his seat. He feels the weight of the "Black Opinion" label he was warned about. He looks around. He sees a few other Black students—one is staring at their laptop, another is giving him a "good luck" look.

LAWRENCE
> (Clearing his throat)
> Well, I think... it’s not just about "soul." It’s about displacement. If you "leverage" a market by pricing out the people who built it, you aren't evolving the community—you’re just replacing it.

The room is quiet. A girl in the front row, TINSLEY (19, White, perfectly manicured), turns around.

TINSLEY
> But isn't that just basic supply and demand? If they can’t afford the "evolution," doesn't the market dictate they move to a more "compatible" tier?

The debate begins to swirl around him, but the Professor keeps looking back to Lawrence as the "arbiter" of the Black experience. Lawrence sighs, doodling a small, jagged line in his notebook. He’s "unsure" if he wants to be the teacher today.

EXT. CAMPUS QUAD - LATER
Lawrence is walking quickly, trying to shake off the lecture energy. He sees AYANA sitting on a stone bench, sketching in a notebook. He heads over.

LAWRENCE
> Tell me the Linguistics building is better than the Business school. Please.

Ayana looks up, closing her sketchbook. She sees the frustration on his face.

AYANA
> Ah, let me guess. You became the "Appointed Representative for All Black People" in Chapter One of your textbook?

LAWRENCE
> (Sitting down)
> It took exactly forty-two minutes. I didn't even get to my granola bar.

AYANA
> It’s a full-time job they don't pay you for. Look, Lawrence... you have a choice. You can either be their encyclopedia, or you can just be a student.

LAWRENCE
> How do I do that when the Professor literally points a spotlight at me?

AYANA
> (Leaning in)
> You start asking them the uncomfortable questions. Flip the script. Make them explain why they think "urban" is a synonym for "cheap."

She stands up, slinging her bag over her shoulder.

AYANA (CONT'D)
> Also, stop wearing those chinos every day. You look like you’re trying to blend into the walls. Stand out. It makes them more nervous.

She starts to walk away, then stops.

AYANA (CONT'D)
> There’s a party tonight. Off-campus. Mostly BSA and some Howard folks. No "Logans" allowed. You coming?

Lawrence feels a spark of the "confidence" returning.

LAWRENCE
> I’ll be there. I’ll even leave the chinos at home.

AYANA
> (Grinning)
> Progress. Text me for the address.

As she walks away, Lawrence’s phone buzzes. It’s a text from CHAD.

C: Logan just asked me if I know where to get "the good greens." I think he means kale, but I’m gonna tell him it’s $100 an ounce. You in?

Lawrence laughs, shaking his head. The "uncomfortable" is still there, but at least he’s not facing it alone.

FADE OUT.

INT. ROW HOUSE - LEADROIT PARK - NIGHT
The house is packed. Bass from a classic 2000s hip-hop track rattles the floorboards. The air smells of cocoa butter, expensive cologne, and wings. This is the "Real D.C."

LAWRENCE stands by the snack table, wearing a crisp black tee and dark denim—no chinos in sight. He looks around, feeling that familiar "in-between" sensation. He’s at a Black party, but he’s still the "Georgetown kid."

CHAD is across the room, mid-conversation with three girls, gesturing wildly. DEREK is in the corner, nodding along to the music while checking his LinkedIn on his phone. TERRY walks up to Lawrence, holding two red cups.

TERRY

TERRY

INT. HARBIN HALL - DORM ROOM - LATER THAT NIGHT (2:30 AM)
The high from the party evaporates the second Lawrence opens the door.

The room is blindingly bright. LOGAN is sitting on his bed, wearing a headlamp and a compression vest, furiously pedaling on his Peloton. The "whirring" sound is deafening in the small space.

On Lawrence’s desk, there is a small, clear baggie containing... dried oregano?

LAWRENCE
> (Shielding his eyes)
> Logan? It’s two in the morning, man.

LOGAN
> (Out of breath)
> "Success doesn't sleep, Lawrence!" Just hitting my zone-four cardio. Hey, thanks for the hookup on the "greens!" Your friend Chad is a lifesaver.

Lawrence looks at the baggie of oregano, then back at Logan.

LAWRENCE
> Logan... Chad sold you that? For how much?

LOGAN
> Fifty bucks! He said it’s "locally sourced" and "very artisanal." I tried to put it in my smoothie, but it tasted a little... Italian?

Lawrence rubs his temples. The "uncomfortable experience" has reached a peak.

LAWRENCE
> Logan, turn off the light. And the bike. We need to talk.

Logan stops pedaling, looking hurt.

LOGAN
> Is this about the "vibe?" I’m trying really hard to be a good roommate, Lawrence. I even looked up a "Black Playlist" on Spotify so you’d feel more at home when you got back.

LAWRENCE
> (Taking a deep breath, finding his voice)
> Okay, first? Don't ever do that. I don't need a "Black Playlist." I have my own ears. Second, Chad is a hustler and he sold you pizza toppings. And third? You can’t ride a stationary bike in a ten-by-ten room at 2 AM while I’m trying to live here.

LOGAN
> I just... I want us to be boys! Like in the movies!

LAWRENCE
> (Stepping into his space, firm but not mean)
> We can be roommates, Logan. But "being boys" requires you to stop treating me like a character in a movie you’ve seen. I’m just a guy trying to get a degree. No more "urban" comments. No more buying fake weed from my friends. And definitely no more headlamps.

Logan looks at his feet, chastened.

LOGAN
> Got it. No headlamps. Can I... can I still keep the Nespresso?

LAWRENCE
> (Sighing)
> Yes. Keep the Nespresso. Just... make me one in the morning. I’m gonna need it.

Lawrence climbs into his bed, staring at the ceiling. He pulls out his phone. A text from Ayana is waiting.

A: You didn't totally embarrass yourself at Spades. See you Monday, Business Man.

A small smile forms on Lawrence’s face. The room is still small, the roommate is still weird, and the school is still intimidating—but for the first time, it feels like he might actually belong in "Northwest."

FADE OUT.


Northwest - Pilot Episode: "The Acceptance" (Finale)
INT. HARBIN HALL - DORM ROOM - MONDAY MORNING
The sun is peeking through the narrow window. The room is quiet—no headlamp, no Peloton whirring.

Lawrence sits up, rubbing his eyes. On his desk sits a steaming cup of Nespresso in a "Georgetown Dad" mug. Next to it is a sticky note: “No headlamp. No fake greens. Just caffeine. - L.”

Lawrence takes a sip. It’s actually good. He catches his reflection in his desk mirror. He looks tired, but the "unsure" look in his eyes is being replaced by a flicker of resolve. He opens his closet, pushes past the khaki chinos, and pulls out a crisp, well-fitted olive green jacket and a clean pair of sneakers.

INT. MCDONOUGH SCHOOL OF BUSINESS - LECTURE HALL - LATER
The atmosphere is the same—stiflingly academic. PROFESSOR WHITTAKER is mid-sentence, leaning against his lectern.

PROFESSOR WHITTAKER
> ...which brings us back to the risk-reward ratio of investing in "high-transition" zones. Lawrence, let’s go back to you. When we talk about "cleaning up" a neighborhood for outside investors, where does the moral hazard lie?

The class turns. Tinsley looks back, pen ready to record the "Black perspective." Lawrence doesn't shift in his seat this time. He leans forward, voice steady.

LAWRENCE
> Before we get to the moral hazard, Professor, I have a question about the premise. Why do we only use terms like "cleaning up" or "revitalization" once the original residents are forced out?

Whittaker blinks, surprised by the pivot.

LAWRENCE (CONT'D)
> If the capital was there all along, why wasn't it "leveraged" to help the people who actually built the culture of that neighborhood? Is the market dictate actually "supply and demand," or is it just the refusal to value Black equity until it's owned by someone else?

A heavy silence hangs in the room. A few Black students in the back row share a glance—some nodding, some looking impressed. Tinsley stops writing.

PROFESSOR WHITTAKER
> (Adjusting his glasses)
> That is... a significant internal critique of the model. Let’s explore that. Class, let's look at "equity" vs. "ownership."

Lawrence exhales. He didn't just answer the question; he changed the conversation. He catches a glimpse of AYANA through the glass door of the lecture hall—she’s walking by, catches his eye, and gives a subtle, two-finger salute.

EXT. GEORGETOWN "HEALY GATES" - LATE AFTERNOON
Music begins to swell—a smooth, bass-heavy track with a D.C. bounce.

FINAL MONTAGE:

CHAD is in the student lounge, leaning over a table of white students in suits. He’s showing them something on his phone, his hands moving fast. He looks like he’s selling them a dream, and they are buying it.

DEREK is in the quietest corner of the library, his desk a masterpiece of organization. He’s highlighted three different textbooks, his face a mask of pure, calculated focus.

TERRY is seen on the Howard University "Yard." He’s surrounded by a sea of Black excellence—Greek life, music, and laughter. He looks at his phone, sends a text, and laughs.

LAWRENCE walks through the Healy Gates, leaving the "Hills" for a bit. He meets the guys at the edge of the campus.

EXT. M STREET - GEORGETOWN - CONTINUOUS
The four of them—Lawrence, Chad, Derek, and Terry—walk down M Street. They are a distinct unit.

CHAD
> So, I just convinced three juniors that I have an "in" at a boutique firm in New York. I don't, but by the time they find out, I will.

DEREK
> That’s fraud, Chad.

CHAD
> That’s "pre-emptive networking," Derek. Keep up.

TERRY
> (To Lawrence)
> How’d the lecture go? Did you tell them the truth or the "Georgetown version"?

LAWRENCE
> I told them the truth. It felt... good.

They stop at a crosswalk. The sun is setting over the Potomac, casting long shadows. Lawrence looks at his friends—the loud one, the smart one, the loyal one—and then back at the imposing clock tower of the school behind him.

He’s still unsure about the next four years. He’s still navigating the "uncomfortable everyday." But as the light changes and the four Black men step off the curb together, it’s clear: they aren't just attending the university; they’re taking it over.

FADE TO BLACK.

Northwest - Episode 7: "The Incident"
EXT. M STREET - GEORGETOWN - NIGHT
The street is alive with the hum of nightlife. Groups of students move in packs. Lawrence, Chad, Derek, and Terry are dressed to impress—not for a gala, but for a night out. Even Lawrence has abandoned the "uniform" for a vintage leather jacket and clean Jordans.

They approach THE TOMBS, a legendary Georgetown basement bar. The line is long, snaking past the brick facade.

LOGAN and two other white students from the Business School are about five people ahead of them in line. Logan sees them and waves enthusiastically.

LOGAN
> (Shouting)
> Lawrence! My guy! Come on up, I’ll tell the guy you’re with us!

Logan speaks to the BOUNCER (mid-30s, burly, indifferent). The bouncer looks at Logan, looks at the guys, and nods. Logan beckons them over.

CHAD
> (Adjusting his jacket)
> See? Logan is finally paying rent in social capital. I take back 10% of the bad things I said about him.

The guys step out of the line and move toward the front. Logan and his friends are ushered inside with a quick nod. Lawrence moves to follow, but the Bouncer puts a heavy arm across the entrance.

BOUNCER
> Whoa, whoa. Where you going?

LAWRENCE
> (Confused)
> We’re with them. Logan just—

BOUNCER
> (Scanning them up and down)
> Not tonight. We’re at capacity.

TERRY
> (Pointing to a group of white girls who just walked in behind Logan)
> Capacity? They just went in. And there’s a line of thirty people behind us.

BOUNCER
> (Voice getting colder)
> I don't like the attitude. And besides— (He points at Terry’s hoodie and Chad’s sneakers) —dress code. No hoodies. No athletic wear.

CHAD
> (Incredulous)
> These are limited edition! They cost more than your car, man! And that dude Logan is literally wearing a fleece vest and boat shoes. How is that not "athletic wear"?

DEREK
> (Stepping in, calm and professional)
> Sir, we are Georgetown students. We have our IDs. If there’s a specific policy we’re violating, we’re happy to step aside, but it seems inconsistent with who you just let in.

BOUNCER
> (Getting in Derek’s face)
> It’s a private establishment. Move along before I call campus PD to clear the sidewalk.

Logan sticks his head back out the door, looking confused.

LOGAN
> Hey, everything okay? Why aren't they coming in?

BOUNCER
> (To Logan)
> They’re not on the list, and they don't meet the code. You want to stay, or you want to leave?

Logan looks at Lawrence, then back at the warm, inviting bar. He looks genuinely distressed but also... hesitant.

LOGAN
> Uh... I mean, I already opened a tab...

Lawrence looks at Logan. The silence is deafening. The "unsure" part of Lawrence wants to tell Logan it’s fine so things don't get awkward. The "confident" part is starting to burn.

LAWRENCE
> (To Logan, quietly)
> Just go back inside, Logan.

Logan disappears back into the bar. The Bouncer smirks.

EXT. POTOMAC WATERFRONT - LATER
The four friends are sitting on a stone wall overlooking the river. The mood is heavy. They’re eating late-night cheesesteaks from a nearby truck.

TERRY
> (Fuming)
> "Capacity." I’m so tired of that word. It’s the polite way of saying "too many of you." We should go back there tomorrow with the BSA. File a formal complaint with the university.

CHAD
> Waste of time. You think the university cares about a bar? I’m more mad at Logan. "I already opened a tab"? That’s your roommate, Lawrence! He should’ve walked out with us.

DEREK
> (Analytical)
> Logan doesn't see it as a choice between us and the bar. He sees it as an unfortunate "misunderstanding" he has nothing to do with. He doesn't have the "skin" in the game to feel the insult.

LAWRENCE
> (Staring at the water)
> What’s worse? The bouncer being a prick, or Logan just... staying?

TERRY
> It’s the same thing, man. One keeps the door shut, the other doesn't care that it’s locked.

CHAD
> This is why I say we stay on our own vibe. Why are we even trying to go to The Tombs? We should’ve been at the spot in Adams Morgan. At least there, the bouncer looks like us.

LAWRENCE
> Because we shouldn't have to go somewhere else, Chad. My dad went here. He told me this was the "Ivy of the South." He told me I’d have the world at my fingertips. He didn't tell me I’d be standing on a sidewalk arguing about my sneakers.

DEREK
> Your dad’s generation thought that getting in was the win. Our generation is realizing that being in the room doesn't mean you’re part of the conversation.

Terry’s phone pings. He looks at it and sighs.

TERRY
> Ayana just texted. She heard about what happened at the door. News travels fast in the BSA. She wants to know if you’re okay, Lawrence.

Lawrence pulls out his own phone. He sees a text from Logan.

L: Sorry about that man. That bouncer was a jerk. I’ll buy you a Nespresso tomorrow to make up for it!

Lawrence looks at the text, then at his friends. He starts typing a response, then deletes it. He looks at the "Georgetown" sweatshirt he’s carrying.

LAWRENCE
> I don't want a Nespresso.

CHAD
> So what do you want?

Lawrence stands up, looking at the distant lights of the university on the hill.

LAWRENCE
> I want to stop feeling like I’m a guest in my own life.

FADE OUT.


Northwest - Episode 8: "The Mecca"
EXT. HOWARD UNIVERSITY - THE YARD - DAY
The energy is electric. It’s Homecoming. The Yard is a sea of blue, white, and red. There’s a drumline battle happening in the distance, the smell of jerk chicken is heavy in the air, and everywhere you look is unapologetic Black joy.

LAWRENCE, CHAD, and DEREK walk through the crowd like they’re navigating a minefield of cool. TERRY walks ahead of them, dapping people up every five feet. He’s wearing a Howard vintage sweatshirt and looks like he’s lived here his whole life.

CHAD
> (Adjusting his designer sunglasses)
> Yo, I feel like my credit score just jumped fifty points just being in the vicinity of this much excellence. Why didn't we come here again?

DEREK
> Because you wanted the "global prestige" of a Georgetown degree, Chad.

CHAD
> (Looking around at a group of girls)
> Prestige is cool, but look at the scenery, Derek. The scenery at Georgetown is... North Face jackets and Patagonia vests. This? This is a music video. I feel like I’m in a Hype Williams set.

LAWRENCE
> (Looking down at his own outfit—a slightly-too-neat polo and slim jeans)
> I feel like I’m wearing a costume. Like I’m "Undercover Freshman."

TERRY

> That’s because you guys smell like "Student Loans" and "Office Hours." Relax. You’re at the Mecca. Nobody’s gonna ask you about your GPA here.

INT. BLACKBURN CENTER - RESTROOM - LATER
Lawrence ducks into the restroom to splash some water on his face. It’s crowded. Guys are checking their fades in the mirror, talkin' loud. Lawrence finds a spot at the end of the long mirror.

He looks at himself. He sees the "Georgetown" lanyard hanging out of his pocket. He tucks it in, then pauses.

[MIRROR LAWRENCE MOMENT]
In the reflection, "Mirror Lawrence" is wearing a full Georgetown suit, holding a Nespresso cup and looking stressed.

MIRROR LAWRENCE
> (Reflecting Lawrence's anxiety)
> Look at you. Trying to blend in. You know you’re gonna go back to the dorm and Logan’s gonna ask you if you "learned any new handshakes."

LAWRENCE
> (Whispering to the mirror)
> Shut up, man. I’m just trying to have a good time.

MIRROR LAWRENCE
> You’re an interloper. A tourist. You’re "Georgetown Lawrence" now. You probably think the bass is too loud.

Lawrence shakes his head, drying his hands aggressively. He exits the restroom and almost runs into AYANA. She looks incredible—braided crown, oversized denim jacket, and a "Black By Popular Demand" tee.

AYANA
> Whoa, Cloud. You look like you just fought a ghost in there.

LAWRENCE
> Just... the mirror. It’s been a long week.

AYANA
> (Leaning in, scanning his face)
> You’re doing that thing again.

LAWRENCE
> What thing?

AYANA
> The "Where do I fit in?" face. You’re at Howard Homecoming, Lawrence. Stop overthinking the "Blackness" of it all and just eat a plate of ribs.

LAWRENCE
> It’s just... Terry fits here. You fit here. I go back to Harbin Hall and my roommate asks me to explain The Wire to him. I feel like I’m losing the version of me that belongs here.

AYANA
> (Softening)
> You aren't losing anything. You’re just expanding. Being "Georgetown Lawrence" doesn't mean you aren't "N.W. Lawrence." It just means you’re the bridge. Bridges are important, bruh. People walk on 'em, but they’re the only way to get across the water.

EXT. THE YARD - MOMENTS LATER
The guys are back together, standing near the Greek plots. MARCUS (the varsity jacket guy from Georgetown) walks up, looking surprisingly relaxed.

MARCUS
> Yo, Lawrence! You made it.

LAWRENCE
> (Surprised)
> Didn't think I’d see you here, Marcus.

MARCUS
> Man, half of the BSA is here. We gotta recharge the batteries before we go back to the "Hilltop." You gotta have a sanctuary, you know?

CHAD
> (To Marcus)
> Hey, you think I could pass for a Howard Junior? I feel like I have the "Mecca" spirit.

MARCUS
> (Laughing)
> Chad, you have "Sales Representative" written all over your forehead. Just stay in your lane, man. The lane is good.

A heavy beat drops—"Dreams and Nightmares" starts playing. The entire Yard erupts.

TERRY
> (Yelling over the music)
> This is it! Don't be "Georgetown" right now, Lawrence! Just be from D.C.!

Lawrence looks at Ayana, who’s already catching the rhythm. He looks at his friends. He finally lets go of the "stiff" posture he’s been carrying all semester. He starts to move, the "unsure" energy fading into the background.

LAWRENCE
> (To himself)
> Bet.

FADE OUT as the camera pans up to the Howard clock tower, then quickly cuts to the Georgetown clock tower—silent, cold, and looming.
